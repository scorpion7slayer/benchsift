# Product

## Register

product

## Users

BenchSift serves developers, technical decision-makers, and AI practitioners who need to narrow a large model catalogue and make an evidence-based choice. They scan rankings quickly, filter by capability or provider, then compare a small shortlist in depth across desktop and mobile contexts.

## Product Purpose

BenchSift brings real model benchmarks, speed, latency, pricing, context, modality, availability, and provider data into one dependable decision surface. Success means a user can find relevant models, understand trade-offs, and share or revisit an exact comparison without fighting the interface or doubting the data.

## Brand Personality

Precise, restrained, pragmatic. The product should feel like a trustworthy technical instrument: calm enough for dense reading, direct in its language, and responsive at every interaction.

## Anti-references

- A wholesale visual redesign that replaces BenchSift's existing warm-neutral, monochrome identity.
- Generic AI SaaS marketing aesthetics, decorative gradients, neon accents, or gratuitous glass effects.
- Fake, seeded, or hard-coded operational data that makes the catalogue look more complete than it is.
- Decorative motion or delayed navigation that makes the product feel slower than the underlying action.
- Desktop-only density that hides core comparison actions behind hover or produces cramped mobile layouts.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence before decoration.** Make real metrics, provenance, and data gaps easy to understand.
2. **The URL is a durable state.** Filters and comparisons that users may revisit or share must navigate predictably and preserve their exact selection.
3. **Dense, then progressively disclosed.** Desktop can show breadth; smaller viewports keep the same information architecture while revealing detail in focused layers.
4. **Immediate interaction feedback.** Selection, navigation, loading, and removal states must respond within a human-perceptible instant.
5. **One visual language.** Reuse the existing semantic tokens, restrained surfaces, Geist typography, and familiar controls across every route and theme.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All core workflows must be keyboard-operable, retain visible focus, meet contrast requirements, expose clear names and states to assistive technology, provide at least 44-by-44-pixel touch targets on coarse pointers, and respect reduced-motion preferences without removing functionality.
